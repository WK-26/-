Executive Summary

Civilization is built by individuals. This paper defines a structural diagnostic model for individuals as “cells” of civilization, using six indices to quantify net contribution or harm: FFI, CSI, CHS, EOI, RUI, and HCCI. It replaces moral labels with structural mechanics and diffusion analysis.

Abstract

This white paper introduces the Individual Civilization Health Check Model (ICHC), a framework to evaluate the structural health of individuals, defining: FFI (constructive capacity), CSI (structural toxicity), CHS (net health = FFI − CSI), EOI (amplification), RUI (virulence of harmful spread), and HCCI (final impact = CHS × RUI). The model provides rubrics, formulas, and safeguards for use in governance, AI alignment, media, and civil society.

Keywords: Civilization, Individual, Immunity, FFI, CSI, CHS, EOI, RUI, HCCI, Kong Weicheng.

Preface — Why an “Individual Civilization Health Check”?

We can measure blood pressure and detect tumors, but we lack diagnostics for structural behavior. Structural “tumors” in language, emotion, and power often precede institutional collapse. This model offers a practical tool to detect functional, silent, and cancerous individuals and to build a civilization immune system.

  • Is this person structurally healthy, silent, or cancerous?
  • Do they possess reverse-unification power that spreads coercive formats?
  • Do they create exponential benefit—or exponential harm?

Chapter 1 — Architecture & Foundational Logic

I. Structural Mechanics, Not Morality

The model assesses structural patterns and diffusion, independent of identity or belief. A person may be “upright” but structurally harmful; or invisible yet structurally vital.

II. Six Indices & Balance Sheet View

  • FFI (assets): constructive capacity.
  • CSI (liabilities): harmful replication.
  • CHS (net equity): $\text{{CHS}} = \text{{FFI}} - \text{{CSI}}$.
  • EOI (amplifier): network leverage.
  • RUI (virulence): nonlinear spread of harm.
  • HCCI (final impact): $\text{{HCCI}} = \text{{CHS}} \times \text{{RUI}}$.

III. Measurement Principles

  • Parsimony and operational definitions.
  • Transparent assumptions and evidence.
  • Calibrate thresholds with data and audits.
  • Diagnostic, not punitive; include appeals.

Chapter 2 — FFI: Functional Fabric Index

FFI asks: “Is this person structurally beneficial to the health and sustainability of civilization?”

Eight dimensions (score each 1–10; FFI = average):

  1. Structural Cognition: power/institutional literacy; language-structure awareness; systems thinking.
  2. Diversity Acceptance: identity inclusion; cross-cultural collaboration; anti-stereotype practice.
  3. Emotional Regulation & Rational Expression: calm discourse; boundaries; evidence-led talk.
  4. Collaboration & Public Participation: trust-building; commons work; transparent process.
  5. Autonomy & Anti-Authoritarian Dependence: independent judgment; principled nonconformity.
  6. Aesthetic & Cultural Creativity: original, emancipatory cultural output; open licensing ethos.
  7. Structural Protection Behavior: defends due process, truth, vulnerable groups; counters disinfo.
  8. Growth & Self-Correction: antifragile learning; feedback; mentoring.

Interpretation: 8–10 high functional; 5–7.9 moderate; ≤4.9 dormant/regressive.

Chapter 3 — CSI: Cancerous Structure Index

CSI measures structural harm potential—patterns that replicate coercion and fragility.

Eight dimensions (score each 1–10; CSI = average):

  1. Structural Absolutism: sacredized hierarchies; single “truth”.
  2. Power Worship: domination over legitimacy; status obsession.
  3. Emotional Manipulation: fear, shame, guilt; gaslighting.
  4. Hierarchical Language: obedience framing; dominance–submission.
  5. Normative Moralism: rigid virtue policing; punishment-first.
  6. Rejection of Pluralism: xenophobia; sectarianism; demonization.
  7. Zero-Sum Logic: win–lose fixation; ends-justify-means.
  8. Structural Replication: recreating oppression across contexts.

Interpretation: 8–10 highly carcinogenic; 5–7.9 latent; 3–4.9 dormant; <3 low-risk.

Chapter 4 — CHS: Civilization Health Score

Definition: net structural health.

Formula: $\text{{CHS}} = \text{{FFI}} - \text{{CSI}}$.

Bands (−9 … +9, normalized): +7–9 deep health; +4–6 evident; +1–3 basic; 0 equilibrium; −1–3 mild drift; −4–6 mid drift; −7–9 deep cancer.

Use: selection, coaching, containment (with due process).

Chapter 5 — EOI: Effectiveness of Influence

EOI quantifies amplification—how far and how deeply a person’s structure propagates.

Model: $\displaystyle \text{{EOI}} = (R\,C\,S\,F\,I\,L) \times T^{{1.3}}$

  • R (Reach: $10^1 … 10^9$), C (0.2–0.9), S (0.1–1.0), F (1–500), I (1–1000), L (1–1000), T (0.8–3.0).

Interpret with CHS: High CHS × High EOI → beacon; Negative CHS × High EOI → threat; High CHS × Low EOI → hidden seed.

Chapter 6 — RUI: Reverse Unification Index

RUI measures the virulence of harmful spread driven by CSI and amplified by EOI.

Let $B = \text{{CSI}} \times \text{{EOI}}$. Then $\displaystyle \text{{RUI}} = B\,\bigl(1 + B^{{0.1}}\bigr)$.

Risk levels (illustrative): Low < $10^4$; Moderate $10^4$–$10^8$; High $10^8$–$10^{12}$; Extreme ≥ $10^{12}$. Always pair with CHS.

Chapter 7 — HCCI: Human Civilization Contribution Index

Final net impact over time.

Formulas: $\text{{HCCI}} = \text{{CHS}} \times \text{{RUI}}$   (equivalently)   $\text{{HCCI}} = (\text{{FFI}}-\text{{CSI}}) \times \bigl[(\text{{CSI}}\,\text{{EOI}})\,(1 + (\text{{CSI}}\,\text{{EOI}})^{{0.1}})\bigr]$.

Bands (tunable): Pillar ≥ +1000; Bystander −10…+10; High-Risk −1000…−100; Core Cancer ≤ −1000.

Chapter 8 — Score Levels & Structural Classifications

  1. Functional Cells: high FFI, low CSI → positive CHS; EOI aligned to cooperation → positive HCCI.
  2. Silent Cells: low activity (FFI/CSI minimal), EOI ≈ 0 → inactive but non-toxic; risk of drift.
  3. Cancerous Individuals: high CSI (often high EOI) → normalize fear/shame/hierarchy; systemic harm.
  4. Civilizational Guardians: high CHS × high EOI → scale de-hierarchization, dignity, and immunity.
  5. High-Risk Spreaders: negative CHS × high EOI → priority containment with transparent safeguards.

Chapter 9 — Practical Applications

  • Governance: selection via CHS/EOI/HCCI; restrict amplification of recurrent negative-HCCI actors (due process).
  • AI Alignment: penalize high-CSI/high-EOI patterns in corpora; embed structural immunity checks.
  • Media & Platforms: down-weight structural pollutants; surface functional voices; diversify inputs.
  • Education & Civil Society: structural literacy; decentralized immune networks; early detection.
  • Organizations: leadership pipelines favor high CHS; postmortems include CSI source analysis.

Safeguards: transparency, appeal rights, independent audits, proportional responses.

Chapter 10 — Future Outlook

  • Structural recognition replaces ideological policing.
  • Everyone holds a structural identity code (CHS, EOI, RUI, HCCI).
  • Politics shifts from identity to structural health design.
  • Survival hinges on millions of structurally awakened individuals.

  • T: graph centrality; adjust exponent post-validation.
